Netflix Content Strategy Analytics

Executive Summary:
This project analyzes Netflix’s content library to uncover patterns in content distribution, genre trends, and global expansion strategy.

The goal was to move beyond simple data exploration and understand how content decisions influence audience engagement and platform growth. By analyzing content type, release trends, ratings, and geographic distribution, the project provides insights into how Netflix structures its content portfolio to attract and retain subscribers.

The findings highlight strategic patterns in content production, timing, and audience targeting that are critical in the competitive streaming industry.

Business Problem:
Streaming platforms like Netflix rely heavily on content strategy to drive:
Subscriber acquisition
User engagement
Retention

However, key questions remain:
What type of content performs best (movies vs TV shows)?
Which genres attract the largest audience?
How does global content distribution affect growth?
When is the best time to release content?

Without data-driven insights, content investment decisions become risky and inefficient.

Methodology:
Data cleaning and preprocessing using Python
Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
Trend analysis (content growth over time)
Genre and rating distribution analysis
Geographic content analysis
Visualization using Matplotlib and Seaborn

Key Metrics
Number of Titles (Movies vs TV Shows)
Content Growth Over Time
Genre Distribution
Country-wise Content Contribution
Content Ratings (Audience targeting)

These are commonly used to evaluate content strategy and audience engagement in streaming platforms

Key Insights
Movies dominate the platform, but TV shows are increasing, indicating a shift toward long-term engagement strategies
A large portion of content is concentrated in a few countries, especially the United States and India
Multi-genre content is common, helping attract diverse audiences
Content additions increased significantly after 2016, showing rapid platform expansion
Mature-rated content (e.g., TV-MA) forms a large portion of the catalog, targeting adult audiences

Studies show Netflix strategically balances global content, genre diversity, and timing to maximize engagement and retention

Business Impact:
Helps understand how content strategy drives subscriber growth
Identifies opportunities for expanding into new regions
Supports better content investment decisions
Highlights the importance of genre diversification and timing

Content strategy is a key driver of user engagement and retention in streaming platforms

Business Recommendations:
Increase investment in TV shows to improve long-term engagement
Expand international content to attract global audiences
Focus on multi-genre content for broader appeal
Optimize content release timing based on audience behavior
Maintain balance between new releases and existing catalog
